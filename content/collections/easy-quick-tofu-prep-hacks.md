## Why You Need to Prep Your Tofu

Tofu straight from the packet is saturated with water. It's essentially a fragile, wet sponge. If you throw it directly into a hot pan, two things happen: 
1. The water immediately releases, turning your stir-fry into a steamed, soggy mess.
2. It actively repels any marinades or sauces you try to apply, resulting in a completely flavourless bite that shatters when you try to flip it.

Traditional pressing takes way too long. When you need a quick, high-protein carb refeed, waiting an hour for a tofu press to do its job isn't viable. Here are the fastest, most effective methods to manipulate the protein structure.

## Method 1: The Microwave Cheat (Max Flavour & Chewy Texture)

This is the ultimate hack for creating a highly porous, chewy, meat-like structure that violently drinks up marinades. 

1. **Slice and Dry:** Wrap the extra-firm tofu block in a towel and press firmly just to remove the surface moisture. Cube it and place it on a microwave-safe plate lined with fresh paper towels.
2. **The Initial Blast:** Microwave the cubes on high for 2 minutes. The intense heat boils the internal water, turning it to steam and forcing it out of the protein matrix.
3. **The Increments:** Check the texture, then continue microwaving in 30-second bursts. You want the surface to look dry but not aggressively cooked or burned.
4. **The Result:** Usually, around the 4-minute mark, you're left with a tight, dense protein sponge. Toss this immediately into sticky sauces like teriyaki or gochujang.

## Method 2: The Boiling Hack (Firm & Lighter Texture)

Boiling tofu sounds counterintuitive—why add water to something you want to dry out?—but the saline heat physically tightens the proteins on the surface of the block.

1. **The Setup:** Bring a small pot of heavily salted water to a vigorous boil.
2. **The Boil:** Drop your extra-firm tofu cubes in and boil for 5–10 minutes.
3. **The Result:** Drain them well. The structure will have tightened significantly, yielding a slightly lighter texture than microwaving but remaining impressively firm. It's perfect for keeping cubes intact during aggressive, high-heat wok cooking and won't disintegrate.

## Method 3: The Raw Reality (What Not to Do)

Skipping the prep entirely (using normal, unpressed tofu) is a guaranteed fail if you want it to carry flavour. Because the internal matrix is already 100% full of its packing water, it physically cannot absorb anything else. It will actively repel any marinades and is almost guaranteed to turn into scrambled mush as soon as you hit it with a spatula.
