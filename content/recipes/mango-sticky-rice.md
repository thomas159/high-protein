tips:
- 'Don''t Skip the Soak: Glutinous rice must soak for 6 hours (or 1 hour in boiling water). Skipping this leaves the core rock-hard.'

- 'Coconut Sponge: Mixing the coconut cream into the rice while it is piping hot allows the grains to swell and lock the rich flavour directly inside, rather than letting the liquid pool at the bottom.'

faq:
- question: Can I use regular white rice or jasmine rice?
  answer: Absolutely not. Glutinous rice has a unique starch structure. Standard rice will just give you a sad, wet pudding.
- question: Why do I need to soak the rice?
  answer: It softens the impenetrable outer shell. If you don't soak it, you'll be chewing on gravel.
steps:
- Rinse the rice until the water runs clear. Soak for 6 hours (or 1 hour in boiling water). Drain and steam for 15-20 minutes until soft and tacky.
- While the rice is piping hot, whisk 80% of the coconut cream with the sugar and a pinch of salt. Fold it into the rice. Cover and rest for 30 minutes so the rice absorbs the milk.
- Heat the remaining 20% of the coconut cream and sugar in a small pan. Stir for 1-2 minutes until completely dissolved into a glossy syrup. Do not boil it or it will split and turn into an oily, granular mess.
- Slice the mango. Plate the sticky rice, arrange the fruit, and heavily drizzle the warm glaze over the top.
