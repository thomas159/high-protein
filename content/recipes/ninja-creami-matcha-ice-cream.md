faq:
- question: Why do I need to make a paste first?
  answer: Matcha is famously tricky to mix into cold liquid. If you dump it straight in, you will get dry, bitter clumps. Whisking it into a paste first with a small splash of liquid ensures a smooth, lump-free base.
- question: Can I use water instead of milk?
  answer: You can, but it will end up icy and thin. Stick to milk to get that rich gelato mouthfeel.
- question: Is there too much caffeine in this?
  answer: It is a potent hit of green tea, so if you are sensitive to caffeine, you might want to scale down the matcha and adjust the sweetener to taste.
steps:
- In a small bowl, add the matcha powder. Pour in a splash of the low-fat milk.
- Whisk thoroughly to form a thick, smooth paste with no dry, bitter lumps. Slowly whisk in a continuous drizzle of milk if needed until it is smooth.
- Pour the matcha paste, the remaining milk, vanilla whey, honey, vanilla extract, and sea salt into a shaker or blender. Whisk or shake thoroughly until completely combined and the sweetener is dissolved.
- Pour the mix into the Creami pint. Do not cross the 'Max Fill' line.
- Freeze on a perfectly flat surface for 24 hours. A slanted freeze will wreck the machine.
- Lock the frozen pint into the Creami outer bowl.
- Hit the 'Lite Ice Cream' button and let it run.
- If it looks powdery (standard for macro ice creams), add a splash of milk and hit 'Re-spin' to force the creamy texture.
- Enjoy immediately. Optional: run the 'mix-in' program with white chocolate chips.
