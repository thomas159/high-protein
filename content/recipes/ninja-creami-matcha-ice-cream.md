faq:
  - question: "Why do I need to make a paste first?"
    answer: "Matcha is notoriously difficult to mix into cold liquids. If you just dump it in, it will form dry, bitter clumps. Making a paste with a small amount of milk ensures a perfectly smooth base."
  - question: "Can I use water instead of milk?"
    answer: "You can, but it will be incredibly icy and lack the dense, creamy mouthfeel. Stick to milk for a proper gelato texture."
  - question: "Is 30g of matcha too much caffeine?"
    answer: "It is a potent dose of matcha! If you are sensitive to caffeine, you might want to halve the matcha and adjust the honey to balance the flavour."

steps:
  - "In a small bowl, add the 30g of matcha powder. Pour in about 50ml of the low-fat milk."
  - "Whisk vigorously to form a thick paste, ensuring there are absolutely no dry, powdery lumps. Slowly whisk in a little more milk if needed until completely smooth."
  - "Pour the matcha paste, the remaining milk, vanilla whey, honey, vanilla extract, and sea salt into a shaker or blender. Blitz or shake violently until completely combined and the honey is dissolved."
  - "Pour the mix into the Creami pint. Do not cross the 'Max Fill' line."
  - "Freeze on a perfectly flat surface for 24 hours. A slanted freeze will wreck the machine."
  - "Lock the frozen pint into the Creami outer bowl."
  - "Hit the 'Lite Ice Cream' button and let it run."
  - "If it looks powdery (standard for macro ice creams), add a splash of milk and hit 'Re-spin' to force the creamy texture."
  - "Enjoy immediately. Optional: run the 'Mix-In' program with white chocolate chips."
