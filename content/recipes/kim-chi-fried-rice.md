faq:
- question: Is day-old rice better than fresh rice for this?
  answer: Yes. Day-old rice is the premium option because the cold, dried-out grains crisp up perfectly. But fresh rice still gets it done in 15 minutes.
- question: Can I make this without Gochujang?
  answer: You can use sriracha mixed with a tiny pinch of sugar and soy sauce, but gochujang is vastly superior.
steps:
- Get your oil smoking in a large wok. Toss in the garlic, veg, and kimchi. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes to caramelise the kimchi.
- Push everything to the edges. Crack the egg into the centre and scramble it fast. Fold the veg back in when the egg is mostly cooked.
- Drop the rice in. Hit it with sesame oil, soy sauce, and gochujang. Blast the heat. Press the rice firmly into the pan for 30 seconds to force a deep, caramelised crust to form, then toss. Repeat to build those elite crispy edges. If you constantly stir, it will steam into a soggy mess instead of crisping.
- Pull it off the heat. Garnish with toasted sesame seeds and spring onions (scallions).
